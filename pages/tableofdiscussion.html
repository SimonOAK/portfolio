Idea

How can the Swedish wood and furniture industry show young talent they are an innovative and world-changing industry to work in – and at the same time be a part of creating a more understanding and connected society? That's what Table of Discussion sets out to do by connecting young first time voters directly to their representatives in the Swedish parliament.

tableofdiscussion.com

Insight and Concept

The number of young people working in the wood and furniture industry is declining, why? Because the industry is looked upon as old fashioned. To change this perception, we set out to show how the industry can be a part of improving something young people are interested in: The future of our democracy. Table of Discussion is a two-parted table, with one part being located in the Swedish parliament, while the other part travels to schools far away from the power. Thus allowing the voices of young concerned first time voters to be heard directly by their representatives.

Results

The interest to be a part of the table sessions where huge from the start – with politicians from all major Swedish parties wanting to be a part of the experience. But most importantly, the interest among young first time voters from schools all over Sweden was even bigger. The table toured from the north to the south, visiting small towns who usually never get a chance to talk to the ones running the country.

This is just the beginning. After the table had visited Almedalen, Sweden's largest political event, we made the table open source. This meant anyone can now build their own table and start conversations and mend divides.
